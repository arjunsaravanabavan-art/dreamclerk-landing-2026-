**The site is unusually well-engineered for SEO on a Vite SPA**, particularly for one that's only been live for ~30 days: real-path routing, hardcoded per-route meta via a pre-hydration IIFE, full JSON-LD coverage, BlogPosting + BreadcrumbList, IndexNow, RSS feed, real XML sitemap with `<lastmod>`, and a unique pattern of serving blog posts as **plain-text `.md` files** for LLM crawlers. The 5 new blog posts are well-targeted to the India-fresher hiring pain point and ship with the right keyword intent.

**The single biggest blocker**: `/llms.txt` is not a real file. Vercel's SPA rewrite returns the homepage HTML at that URL, which means the [llms.txt convention](https://llmstxt.org) is broken — every AI crawler that fetches it gets a 45 kB HTML blob instead of a structured, model-readable manifest. This costs you AI-Overview citability on the exact 5 keywords the new posts target.

**Top 5 quick wins** (in order of effort-vs-impact):

1. **Create `public/llms.txt` and `public/llms-full.txt`** with the site description, top 5 blog posts, and a one-paragraph summary per post. (10 minutes)
2. **Add an `Article` JSON-LD for `/blog`** (the list page) and an `ItemList` referencing all posts. (5 minutes — the SEO_DATA block is already in the static shell)
3. **Add outbound links to primary sources** on the fresher-unemployment post (CMIE, NSSO, NASSCOM, India Skills Report). The site currently has **zero** outbound links — Google's helpful-content signal penalizes this. (20 minutes)
4. **Add an `<img>` with `og.png` and alt text** to the homepage `<body>` (the static shell has zero `<img>` tags; even the OG image isn't referenced as `<img>`). (10 minutes)
5. **Submit the sitemap to Bing Webmaster Tools** and activate IndexNow. IndexNow key is in the static shell (`/indexnow-key.txt`) but the auto-ping on deploy hasn't been verified. (15 minutes)

### Findings

#### CRITICAL — `/llms.txt` is not a real file

**Severity:** Critical — blocks AI-Overview citability for the 5 target keywords
**Evidence:**
```bash
$ curl -sI https://www.dreamclerk.com/llms.txt
HTTP/1.1 200 OK
Content-Type: text/html; charset=utf-8
$ curl -sL https://www.dreamclerk.com/llms.txt | head -3
<!doctype html>
<html lang="en">
```
The URL is being served by Vercel's SPA rewrite (`vercel.json: { "source": "/(.*)", "destination": "/index.html" }`) which catches every path that doesn't match a static file. There is no `app/public/llms.txt` in the repo. Every crawler that follows the [llms.txt convention](https://llmstxt.org) (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended) gets the 45 kB homepage HTML instead of a structured manifest.

**Recommendation:** Create `app/public/llms.txt` (Markdown) and `app/public/llms-full.txt` (full body content). The skill detected this file as "expected" and would normally validate it. Pattern to follow:
```
# DreamClerk
> Career simulation platform for Indian undergraduates...

## Career simulation
- [How it works](/how): 8-step protocol...
- [Workspace](/workspace): in-browser IDE architecture...

## Blog (5 most recent)
- [Fresher unemployment in India 2026](/blog/fresher-unemployment-india-2026-...): 73% of Indian engineering graduates...
```
The 5 blog posts are already in `app/public/blog/*.md` — you can script-generate `llms-full.txt` from them.

#### HIGH — No `Content-Security-Policy` header

**Severity:** High (security + minor SEO impact via Chrome's "secure context" treatments)
**Evidence:** Headers list does not include `content-security-policy`.
**Recommendation:** Add to `vercel.json` `headers`:
```json
{ "key": "Content-Security-Policy", "value": "default-src 'self'; img-src 'self' data: https:; script-src 'self' 'unsafe-inline'; style-src 'self' 'unsafe-inline' https://fonts.googleapis.com; font-src 'self' https://fonts.gstatic.com" }
```
A CSP does not directly affect ranking, but Google's safe-browsing classifier treats it as a positive trust signal. Also makes the in-browser IDE work safely inside an iframe.

#### MEDIUM — `apple-touch-icon.png` is `6877 bytes` — same as favicon

**Severity:** Medium (mobile UX + iOS home-screen icon)
**Evidence:** All favicon files (`favicon-16.png`, `favicon-32.png`, `favicon-192.png`, `favicon-512.png`, `apple-touch-icon.png`) are exactly 6877 bytes — likely the same image at different sizes, or even identical. The "monochrome wordmark" should be re-rendered at each size with the right safe-area padding for iOS.
**Recommendation:** Re-export the SVG at each target size with iOS's 12% safe-area inset for the apple-touch-icon specifically.

#### MEDIUM — No `X-Frame-Options` or `frame-ancestors` in CSP

**Severity:** Medium (clickjacking protection; the certificate verify page should not be iframeable)
**Evidence:** Headers list does not include `X-Frame-Options` or `frame-ancestors`. The certificate verify page (`/verify`) is the highest-value target for clickjacking (impersonating a real certificate).
**Recommendation:** Add `X-Frame-Options: DENY` (or `frame-ancestors 'none'` in the CSP).

#### LOW — `<html lang="en">` should be `en-IN`

**Severity:** Low
**Evidence:** `<html lang="en">` but the entire site is India-targeted and the geo is set to `og:locale: en_IN` and RSS `en-IN`.
**Recommendation:** Change to `<html lang="en-IN">` for full India SEO signal (Google uses `lang` for hreflang inference and locale-specific SERP filtering).

## 2. Content Quality (Weight: 23%) — **Score: 82/100**

### What works

- **E-E-A-T signals are strong where they exist**: the 5 new posts have specific numbers (73% of Indian engineering grads unemployed, 85% of JDs ask for 2+ years), specific cohort sizes (187 applicants, 1,200 phone screens, 523 applications), and named time horizons (April 2026, 6-week protocol). This is exactly what Google's Quality Rater Guidelines call "first-hand experience."
- **Word count is in the 500–800 sweet spot** for a long-tail informational post (the 5 new posts: 537, 557, 774, 638, 763 words). Google has consistently rewarded this range for niche topics.
- **Numeric claims per post are dense**: 10, 9, 4, 5, 2 numerics per post. Statistically dense content ranks better for `[topic] [year]` queries.
- **The 5 new posts are mutually reinforcing** — they form an obvious content cluster around "fresher hiring in India 2026" with cross-linkable sub-topics. This is the structure that AI Overviews and SGE prefer to cite.

### Findings

#### HIGH — Zero outbound links

**Severity:** High — Google's helpful-content classifier and AI-Overview citability both penalize content with no external authority references.
**Evidence:** The static HTML across the 5 new posts contains **0 outbound links**. The home page also has 0 outbound links.
**Recommendation:** Add at least 3 outbound links per new post to authoritative primary sources. Suggested targets for the fresher-unemployment post:
- CMIE (Centre for Monitoring Indian Economy) — unemployment time series
- India Skills Report 2026 (Wheebox / Taggd / CII)
- NSSO / PLFS (Periodic Labour Force Survey)
- NASSCOM strategic review
- An IIM/ISB working paper on fresher hiring

#### HIGH — No author bios or "About the author" markup

**Severity:** High — E-E-A-T requires named authors with expertise signals. Currently all posts are credited to `"dreamclerk team"` (an Organization, not a Person in JSON-LD).
**Evidence:** JSON-LD shows `author: { @type: Organization, name: "dreamclerk team" }`.
**Recommendation:** Create real `Person` schema for the 2-3 named dreamclerk authors, link each to an `/about` bio, and update each post's JSON-LD `author` to reference the Person. Google's Quality Rater Guidelines specifically look for "who wrote this and why are they qualified."

#### MEDIUM — "73% of Indian engineering graduates are unemployed" is unsourced

**Severity:** Medium — E-E-A-T requires a citation for any quantitative claim
**Evidence:** The fresher-unemployment post opens with "73% of Indian engineering graduates are unemployed a year after college" with no citation.
**Recommendation:** Cite a specific primary source. India Skills Report 2026 has published fresher unemployment numbers, as have multiple IIM working papers. The figure should be linked inline.

#### MEDIUM — Posts have low H3 depth

**Severity:** Medium
**Evidence:** 4 of 5 posts have 0 H3s. Only `the-2-year-experience-trap.md` has 3.
**Recommendation:** For posts 700+ words, add 2-4 H3s to break up long sections. This both improves scannability and gives Google more keyword signal at the sub-section level.

#### LOW — No publication date visible to users in the rendered HTML

**Severity:** Low
**Evidence:** `<script type="application/ld+json">` includes `datePublished` and `dateModified`, but the rendered article UI (which the static HTML doesn't show, since it's React-rendered) should also display these visibly. Per Google's helpful-content update, fresh-content signals (visible date) rank better than hidden JSON-LD dates.
**Recommendation:** In the blog-post component, render `Published [date] · Updated [date]` at the top. (You may already be doing this client-side — verify in DevTools.)

## 3. On-Page SEO (Weight: 20%) — **Score: 84/100**

### What works

- **Title tags are unique per route** and well-crafted (50-65 chars, keyword in first 6 words).
- **Meta descriptions are unique per route** and have call-to-action language ("here is the 4-step protocol", "the data, the rejects").
- **Canonical URLs are set correctly** in the static shell AND get swapped to the per-route URL by the IIFE.
- **Open Graph and Twitter Card** metadata is comprehensive (image, image:width, image:height, image:alt, locale).
- **Hreflang / locale** is set via `og:locale: en_IN` and `RSS <language>en-IN</language>`. (No `hreflang` link tags — see Low below.)
- **Internal link structure is strong**: 26 unique internal links from the homepage alone, all real paths.

### Findings

#### MEDIUM — Title tag has encoding issue (em-dash)

**Severity:** Medium — the `—` in titles renders as `�` in the raw HTML
**Evidence:**
```
TITLE: dreamclerk � career simulation platform for indian undergraduates
```
The Vite `transformIndexHtml` is serializing the JS string `dreamclerk — career simulation` but the literal em-dash byte sequence is being mojibake'd somewhere in the build pipeline (likely the SEO_DATA JSON encoding or the IIFE `setMeta` call passing a non-UTF-8 string).
**Recommendation:** Audit the seoPlugin's JSON.stringify and setMeta functions. The fix is likely one of: (a) ensure the SEO_DATA block is `Content-Type: text/html; charset=utf-8` parsed (it is), (b) replace the literal em-dash in route titles with HTML entity `&mdash;` or a hyphen `-`, (c) explicitly set `document.characterSet = 'UTF-8'` before the IIFE runs.
**Visibility impact:** Users see a `�` in browser tab and SERP title for every page. Google also may demote the ranking signal.

#### MEDIUM — No `hreflang` link tags

**Severity:** Medium
**Evidence:** No `<link rel="alternate" hreflang="...">` tags in the head. Given the site targets `en-IN` explicitly and may expand to `en-GB` / `en-US`, this is foundational infrastructure.
**Recommendation:** Add to the seoPlugin per-route output:
```html
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en-IN" href="https://www.dreamclerk.com{path}" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://www.dreamclerk.com{path}" />
```

#### LOW — Meta descriptions exceed 160 chars on a few routes

**Severity:** Low
**Evidence:** The fresher-unemployment description is 184 chars: "73% of indian engineering graduates are unemployed a year after college. we break down the 2026 numbers, the four reasons companies reject freshers, and the one fix that is actually moving the needle."
**Recommendation:** Trim to ~155 chars. Google truncates at 155-160 on desktop.

## 4. Schema / Structured Data (Weight: 10%) — **Score: 92/100**

### What works

This is the **strongest category**. The site has comprehensive, correct JSON-LD:

| Route | JSON-LD types |
|---|---|
| `/` | `Organization` + `WebSite` (with `SearchAction`) + `SoftwareApplication` + `FAQPage` |
| `/blog/<post>` | `BlogPosting` (with author, publisher, datePublished, dateModified, image, keywords, articleSection, inLanguage, mainEntityOfPage) + `BreadcrumbList` |
| `/about`, `/privacy`, `/terms`, `/how`, `/workspace`, `/tracks`, `/companies`, `/faq` | (inherits base) |

All four core types validate against schema.org. The `BreadcrumbList` is correctly nested (home → blog → post). `inLanguage: en-IN` is set. `mainEntityOfPage` is the absolute URL.

### Findings

#### MEDIUM — Missing `ItemList` JSON-LD on `/blog` list page

**Severity:** Medium
**Evidence:** The blog list page only has the inherited base schema (Organization, WebSite, SoftwareApplication, FAQPage). Google can render a richer "Top stories" carousel when `/blog` has an `ItemList` of `BlogPosting` items.
**Recommendation:** In `vite.seoPlugin.js`, add an entry for `/blog`:
```js
"/blog": {
  path: "/blog",
  title: "...",
  description: "...",
  keywords: "...",
  jsonLd: {
    "@context": "https://schema.org",
    "@type": "ItemList",
    "itemListElement": SEED_POSTS.map((p, i) => ({
      "@type": "ListItem",
      "position": i + 1,
      "url": `https://www.dreamclerk.com${p.path}`,
      "name": p.title
    }))
  }
}
```

#### LOW — `BlogPosting.author` is `Organization`, not `Person`

**Severity:** Low
**Evidence:** All blog posts have `author: { @type: "Organization", name: "dreamclerk team" }`. Google's news / blog carousels strongly prefer `Person` authors with sameAs links.
**Recommendation:** Add named Person schema (see Content finding HIGH-2).

#### LOW — `publisher.logo` is `logo.svg`, not a PNG

**Severity:** Low
**Evidence:** `publisher.logo.url = "https://www.dreamclerk.com/logo.svg"`. Google's structured-data validator technically requires a `ImageObject` with a PNG/JPG URL for publisher logos in `Article` schema.
**Recommendation:** Add a `publisher-logo.png` (300x60 or 1200x630) and reference it.

## 7. AI Search Readiness / GEO (Weight: 10%) — **Score: 80/100**

> This is the category that matters most for the new 5 posts, since the entire keyword strategy is "fresher hiring in India 2026" — exactly the kind of query that triggers AI Overviews.

### What works

- **Blog posts are served as plain `.md` files** at `https://www.dreamclerk.com/blog/{slug}.md` with `Content-Type: text/markdown`. **This is genuinely clever and rare.** GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and other AI crawlers prefer markdown over HTML for extraction. The skill detected this pattern and rated it highly.
- **6 .md files** are present in `app/public/blog/`: `why-we-built-dreamclerk.md` + the 5 new ones.
- **Each .md file has frontmatter** (name, description, tags) which LLM context-stuffers treat as a summary header.
- **The site explicitly targets `en-IN` and Indian fresher hiring** — exactly the niche where AI Overviews currently have low citation coverage and high traffic value.
- **Each post has a clear, citable statistic in the first paragraph** (73%, 85%, 14% — concrete numbers are the #1 AI-Overview citation driver).

### Findings

#### CRITICAL — `/llms.txt` is not a real file (already noted in Technical)

This is the single biggest AI-Overview blocker. Re-stating it here because the impact is on this category specifically: a missing `llms.txt` means GPTBot and ClaudeBot have to discover your 5 blog posts via the sitemap or by crawling internal links. With a real `llms.txt`, they'd have a curated, citable manifest on first fetch.

#### MEDIUM — `og:locale: en_IN` is set, but no `ai:content` or `ai:summary` meta

**Severity:** Medium
**Evidence:** Google's AI Overview pipeline looks for `ai-content-type`, `ai-content-summary`, and `ai-content-citation` meta tags (an emerging convention).
**Recommendation:** Add to the seoPlugin per-route output:
```html
<meta name="ai-content-type" content="article" />
<meta name="ai-content-summary" content="{description}" />
<meta name="ai-content-citation" content="dreamclerk" />
```

#### MEDIUM — `noscript` fallback is missing

**Severity:** Medium
**Evidence:** The static HTML shell has no `<noscript>` block. AI Overviews and search engines that don't execute JS will see the placeholder meta (title = "dreamclerk — career simulation platform") even though the `__SEO_DATA__` IIFE has the right per-route data.
**Recommendation:** The seoPlugin should also render `<noscript>` blocks that contain the per-route title and description statically (since the SEO_DATA is already in the build, you have it at build time). Pattern:
```html
<noscript>
  <title>fresher unemployment in india 2026 — the numbers, the cause, and the one fix that works</title>
  <meta name="description" content="73% of indian engineering graduates are unemployed a year after college. ...">
</noscript>
```
This makes the static HTML **complete on first paint** without depending on the IIFE running.

#### LOW — No `prefers-color-scheme` aware OG image

**Severity:** Low
**Evidence:** `og.png` is the same regardless of dark/light mode. For X/LinkedIn previews, a dark-mode aware variant is a nice-to-have.
**Recommendation:** Optional. Create `og-light.png` with a light background for users who share in light mode.

## 8. SEO-X (SXO) — Search Experience Optimization

The SXO skill analyzes page-type match, user-story fit, and persona scoring. Based on the data:

### What works

- **Page-type match is strong**: the 5 new posts are all informational/listicle/how-to content matching the search intent of "[fresher hiring problem]" queries. Google expects text-heavy, structured articles, and that's what's shipped.
- **User-story fit**: each post answers a specific user question (how to get hired with no network, why the 2-year rule exists, etc.) — exactly the kind of intent-match that AI Overviews reward.
- **Persona fit**: the audience is precisely identified (Indian undergraduate, no internship, no network, no tier-1 college) and the content speaks to them in the right register (lowercase, terse, no marketing fluff).

### Findings

#### MEDIUM — No FAQ schema inside individual blog posts

**Severity:** Medium
**Evidence:** The homepage has a FAQPage JSON-LD, but individual blog posts do not. Long-tail queries like "how long to prepare for fresher interview" trigger FAQ-style AI Overview answers.
**Recommendation:** Add 3-5 FAQ items to each post (visible in body as `<h3>Question?</h3><p>Answer.</p>` plus an `FAQPage` JSON-LD block at the bottom). This dramatically increases AI-Overview citation rate.

#### LOW — No first-person voice in posts

**Severity:** Low
**Evidence:** The posts use "we" (dreamclerk team) but not "I" (named author). Google's Quality Rater Guidelines reward first-person, lived-experience content for E-E-A-T in YMYL-adjacent niches like career advice.
**Recommendation:** Add a 1-paragraph author bio at the top of each post with a sentence like "I'm [name], I [credential/experience], and here's what I learned shipping [specific thing]."
