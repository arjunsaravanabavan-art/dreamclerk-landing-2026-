§ 03.7 — Frequently asked

The questions you'll ask first.

001 What is DreamClerk?

DreamClerk is a real-world engineering track for undergraduate students in India. You'll get hired into a fictional company, ship code in an in-browser IDE (Monaco + Docker + Jupyter), get reviewed by an AI senior engineer, and earn a verified work record — not a completion badge, but a recruiter-checkable work record backed by your actual code submissions.

002 How is DreamClerk different from LeetCode?

LeetCode tests algorithm recall in isolated problems. DreamClerk puts you inside a fictional engineering company with a codebase, a tech lead, a PR queue, and 5pm deadlines. The output is a verified work record recruiters can scan, not a puzzle score.

003 What does a DreamClerk certificate look like?

Each certificate contains your name, role title, seniority level, and the fictional company you worked at. It also contains your actual code submissions, AI review scores, sprint velocity, and a capstone summary. It is blockchain-anchored and QR-verifiable by any recruiter.

004 How long does a DreamClerk sprint take?

Each sprint is 8 weeks. You'll work as an Intern or Junior engineer inside a fictional company, ship real code, get reviewed, and complete a capstone. After three sprints at a level, plus a technical interview and capstone, you earn your certificate.

005 Is DreamClerk hiring right now?

DreamClerk is invite-only and will let in its first cohort soon. Drop your email below to get the next letter — one email only, when applications open.

006 How much does DreamClerk cost?

Pricing is still being finalized. The first cohort will get lifetime access at founder rates. No payment is taken at sign-up — only when seats open.
